Latin
Sinotype

This system is a stroke-based typing interface that reimagines the Latin alphabet as a set of constructed forms rather than fixed keys. Instead of typing letters directly, users input sequences of strokes. The system compares these inputs against predefined stroke orders for each letter (A–Z), predicts the closest match, and visually builds the letter in real time within a preview canvas.

Esoteric Typesetting Tool

The tool operates at the intersection of typesetting and esoteric coding, where letterforms are not predefined but executed as sequences of strokes. By treating typographic construction as a rule-based system, it transforms writing into a form of encoding—one that oscillates between legibility and abstraction.

How to Use
01
Press stroke keys to build a letter

Keys 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 each map to one of 7 primitive stroke types — vertical, horizontal, two diagonals, and three arc types. Press them in any combination. Each keypress adds a stroke to the active glyph cell, drawn instantly in its assigned color. Order does not matter, only which strokes you've entered.

02
Watch candidates update live

As you enter strokes, a dropdown candidate list appears below the input area, updating in real time. It shows up to 9 matching letters. The closest match is highlighted in blue. Ghost strokes in the glyph preview show what's still needed to complete the selected letter.

03
Commit a letter

Press Space or Enter to commit the highlighted candidate. Use + and − to navigate candidates, or press ↵ Enter to commit the highlighted one. The letter is added to your text output on the right.

04
Build words and sentences

Press * with no strokes entered to insert a word gap. Use Backspace to remove the last stroke, or delete the last committed letter. Repeat letter by letter to compose full words and sentences in the output field.

Alphabet

To better understand the structure of the Latin alphabet, each letter is broken down into its fundamental strokes and highlighted in different colors. Color-coding isolates recurring elements and reveals how they combine to form distinct letterforms.

Download Extension

Use Latin Sinotype anywhere in Chrome — in search bars, Google Docs, text fields, anywhere you type. The extension intercepts your number keys and converts stroke sequences directly into letters inline.

Installation
1 Download the .zip file below
2 Open Chrome and go to chrome://extensions
3 Enable Developer Mode (toggle top-right)
4 Unzip the file, then click Load unpacked and select the sinotype-extension folder
5 Click the LS icon in your toolbar to activate on any page
